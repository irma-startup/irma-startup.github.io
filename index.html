Low Voltage Networks
At the core of energy transition & smart grids

So far, efforts focused on the transmission networks, which infrastructures are most critical. Low Voltage networks remain poorly known , and their study is challenging: their millions of kilometers of lines have never been instrumented ; and the available information is limited and often outdated.

Today, this lack of knowledge negatively affects the deployment of smart grids. As an example, it is difficult to predict the impact of a new photovoltaic system or electric vehicle.

Our Innovation
Take advantage of smart meters data to shed light on the low voltage network

We pair smart grids studies with sophisticated big data analytics to build maps with physical meaning.

These maps are of great value for the operation and maintenance of the electrical distribution network. They can greatly simplify the network operation procedure by allowing advanced electrical simulations to be performed.

Customer Value
A set of services simplyfing network operation.

We assist our client with their collection of smart-meters data in order to build a digital map of their network.

These services are designed to make the best of the existing equipments : reduction of the need for reinforcements, relief of the constraints, and help to respect the electricity quality norms.

The Team

Clémentine Benoit
CEO, Cofounder

Clémentine holds a MSc in Power Systems and Associated Markets from Grenoble Institute of Technology, and defended a PhD from the University of Grenoble-Alpes at G2Elab, on which she focused on Low Voltage Smart Grids. During her PhD she engaged in the KIC InnoEnergy PhD school program, where she received trainings in innovation and entrepreneurship. She continued her research as a postdoctoral fellow at Schneider Electric.

She specialized in distribution networks identification, optimization, state estimation and load forecasting, and therefore she has a global understanding of the electrical system. She worked closely with three distribution network operators (ERDF and GEG in France, TECTEO in Belgium), which has helped her to understand the complexity of this sector.
