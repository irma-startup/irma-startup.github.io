What we do at irma*

Automated digitalization of distribution networks using smart meters.
Only smart meters data. No additional sensor. No paper archives.

Smart meters data are collected and analyzed to estimate the network physical characteristics and topology. The network is discovered and digitalized, without additional sensors or information being required. Digitalization allows advanced electrical studies to be performed very easily.

Why distribution networks ?

Distribution networks are much trickier.

The transmission system is well known.

It is extremely reliable and efficient.

But distribution networks are not.

Their operation and planning are therefore difficult to optimize.
This is even truer for Low Voltage networks, which are largely unkown.

200 millions smart meters will be deployed in Europe by 2020.

This opens the first opportunity to enlighten Low Voltage networks.

The Team

Clémentine Benoit
CEO, Cofounder

Clémentine holds a MSc in Power Systems and Associated Markets from Grenoble Institute of Technology, and defended a PhD from the University of Grenoble-Alpes, on which she focused on Low Voltage Smart Grids. During her PhD she engaged in the KIC InnoEnergy PhD school program, where she received trainings in innovation and entrepreneurship.

She specialized in distribution networks identification, optimization, state estimation and load forecasting, and therefore she has a global understanding of the electrical system. She worked closely with three distribution network operators (ERDF and GEG in France, TECTEO in Belgium), which has helped her to understand the complexity of this sector.
